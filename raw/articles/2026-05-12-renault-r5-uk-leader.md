## Summary

Renault 5 E-Tech electric became the UK's best-selling electric car in April 2026, according to SMMT registration data. This milestone highlights a sharp shift in consumer demand as rising fuel costs accelerate the transition to electric mobility.

## Key Data Points

### Sales Performance
- Renault 5 E-Tech: UK's #1 best-selling EV in April 2026
- Renault EV lineup (R5, R4, Megane, Scenic): 49% of brand's total passenger car registrations in April
- Online enquiries increased 42% following oil price spike in late February

### Technical Specifications
- Range: Up to 252 miles (WLTP)
- Fast charging: 15% to 80% in ~30 minutes
- Pricing: Starts from £21,495 (OTR, with UK Electric Car Grant)

### New Features (May 2026)
- One-pedal driving (4th level regenerative braking) - standard on higher trims
- Driver attention alert system (interior camera for fatigue/distraction detection)
- "PoweR5" decals for customization
- OpenR Link infotainment with Google built-in
- Up to 2GB monthly data for in-car apps

### Market Context
- Annual savings vs petrol: ~£650 for average driver
- Rising fuel costs driving EV transition
- Rapidly expanding charging network

## Signal Significance

This is a **bellwether event** - not winning on price alone (£21,495 is competitive but not the cheapest), but on design + pricing balance. The 49% EV share of brand sales shows Renault's electrification transition is working, though pressure on ICE sales is intensifying.
